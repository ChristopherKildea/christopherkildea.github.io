Real-Time Hit Display

Program created to aid with the calibration of our robot’s shooter on my RMUL robotics team. The IR sensor (right) is shot at by the robot. The program continuously takes in coordinate info from this data and employs Python’s GraphQl to display it in the correct relative position on the monitor. This gives us a visual representation of where the shots are hitting. The program also calculates the rate of fire and distribution of shots. It sends this data over the network using ROS 2’s networking abilities so the shooter can recalibrate itself.

Python, ROS 2, GraphQl, Multithreading
